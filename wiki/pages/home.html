A Fragmented Research Landscape

Literature reviews span years of work across many papers and venues. Claims are expressed in different ways, evidence appears in different places, and contradictions are rarely obvious. Understanding the state of knowledge still depends heavily on patient, manual synthesis.

"Doing research in big 26 is very tuff indeed"

A Double-Edged Sword

Access to research has never been easier.

Open archives, preprints, and digital libraries have transformed how knowledge is shared. Researchers today can access decades of work with a few clicks.

But this abundance comes with a cost. As the volume of literature grows, keeping track of what has been shown, what is assumed, and where results disagree becomes increasingly difficult.

"So, where do I apply for that quant job?"

Comprehensive tool to support research

Making research easier to understand, and faster to produce

Our goal is not to replace reading, but to support it. We focus on helping researchers see the structure that is already present in the literature such as claims, evidence, assumptions, and connections.

Instead of treating papers as isolated documents, we treat them as sources of related ideas that can be compared, linked, and explored.

  • Identify key ideas without scanning entire papers.
  • See which pieces of evidence support which claims.
  • Notice and visualise overlaps, gaps, and disagreements across papers.
